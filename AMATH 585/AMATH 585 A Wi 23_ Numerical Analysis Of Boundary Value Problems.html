AMATH 585 Winter 2023 Numerical analysis

Class Time: MWF 2:30-3:20pm in HRC 135

Instructor: Prof. Jingwei Hu  

Email: hujw@uw.edu   

Office Hours: Wednesdays 3:30-5:00pm (Lewis 313)

Course Description: Numerical approximation and interpolation. Numerical differentiation and integration. Iterative methods for solving nonlinear systems of equations. Numerical methods for ordinary differential equations. Fast Fourier transform and operator spectrum approximation (if time permits).

Prereq: AMATH 584 recommended, AMATH 352 or equivalent required. AMATH 581 or an undergrad numerical analysis course will do.

Textbook: We will mainly follow the book "Numerical Analysis" (second edition) by Walter Gautschi. A PDF version of the book can be downloaded from https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-0-8176-8259-0 

Homework: Homework assignments will be posted on Gradescope. Homework problems will include both theoretical and coding problems. It is important that you show your work to receive partial credit. For problems that require coding, attach also the script (MATLAB recommended) that you used to generate the results. No late homework will be accepted.

Exams: There will be one midterm and one final. Both exams will be closed-book. No calculators are allowed. One crib sheet in the form of one letter-sized paper hand-written on one or both sides is allowed in the midterm. Two such sheets are allowed in the final exam. The midterm will cover up through (including) Lecture 12. The final will be cumulative.

  Midterm (in class), Monday, February 13, location: HRC 135

  Final Exam, Tuesday, March 14, 2:30-4:20pm, location: HRC 135

Grading Policy: Your final grade will be calculated based on the following percentage

  Homework 50%     Midterm 20%     Final 30%

The average of the class will get roughly a 3.7 GPA. 

Useful References: 

  • Numerical Mathematics, A. Quarteroni, R. Sacco and F. Saleri.
  • Numerical Mathematics and Computing, W. Cheney and D. Kincaid.
  • A First Course in Numerical Methods, U. Ascher and C. Greif.
  • Finite Difference Methods for Ordinary and Partial Differential Equations, R. LeVeque.
